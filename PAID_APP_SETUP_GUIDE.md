# Setting Up ChessBoard as a Paid App

## Overview

Making your app paid requires additional setup in Google Play Console, including payment processing, tax information, and merchant account configuration.

## Key Differences: Free vs Paid App

### Free App
- ✅ No payment setup needed
- ✅ Users download immediately
- ✅ Higher download potential
- ❌ No direct revenue

### Paid App
- ✅ Direct revenue per download
- ✅ Higher perceived value
- ❌ Requires payment setup
- ❌ Requires tax information
- ❌ Lower download volume typically
- ❌ Users see price before downloading

## Step-by-Step: Converting to Paid App

### Step 1: Set Up Google Play Merchant Account

**Required before you can sell apps:**

1. **Go to:** Play Console → Settings → Payments → Merchant account
2. **Click:** "Set up merchant account"
3. **Fill in required information:**
   - Business name (or personal name)
   - Business address
   - Tax information (varies by country)
   - Bank account details for payouts
   - Contact information

**Important:**
- This is a **one-time setup** per developer account
- You'll need to provide tax information (TIN/EIN in US, VAT in EU, etc.)
- Google will verify your information (can take 1-3 days)
- You need a bank account to receive payments

**Tax Information Requirements:**
- **US Developers:** Tax ID (SSN or EIN)
- **EU Developers:** VAT number
- **Other Countries:** Check Google's tax requirements for your country

---

### Step 2: Set Pricing When Creating App

**When creating your app in Step 4 of the submission process:**

1. **Go to:** Play Console → Create app
2. **Fill in app details:**
   - App name: `ChessBoard`
   - Default language: English
   - App or game: Game
3. **Select pricing:**
   - **Choose:** "Paid" instead of "Free"
   - **Set price:** Select your price tier
   - **Price tiers:** $0.99, $1.99, $2.99, $4.99, $9.99, etc.
   - **Currency:** Automatically set based on country

**Price Tier Options:**
- Minimum: $0.99 USD (or equivalent)
- Common tiers: $0.99, $1.99, $2.99, $4.99, $9.99
- You can set different prices for different countries
- Prices are shown in local currency automatically

**Recommended Pricing for ChessBoard:**
- **$0.99** - Entry-level, competitive pricing
- **$1.99** - Mid-range, good value perception
- **$2.99** - Premium positioning
- **$4.99+** - Premium/niche market

### Step 4: Set Up Pricing & Distribution (Updated)

**Go to:** Play Console → Your App → Pricing & distribution

**For Paid Apps:**

1. **Pricing:**
   - Set base price (e.g., $1.99)
   - Set country-specific prices (optional)
   - Enable promotional pricing (optional - for sales)

2. **Distribution:**
   - Select countries/regions
   - Note: Some countries may have restrictions on paid apps

3. **Tax Information:**
   - Already set up in Step 1 (Merchant Account)
   - Google handles tax collection automatically
   - You receive net revenue after Google's 30% commission

**Google's Commission:**
- **30%** of sale price goes to Google
- **70%** goes to you
- Example: $1.99 app → You get ~$1.39 per sale

---

### Step 5: Payment Processing Setup

**Google handles all payment processing automatically:**

✅ **What Google Handles:**
- Payment processing
- Currency conversion
- Tax collection (in most countries)
- Refund processing
- Payment security

✅ **What You Need:**
- Merchant account (Step 1)
- Valid bank account
- Tax information

**Payment Timeline:**
- Users pay immediately when purchasing
- Google processes payments
- You receive payouts monthly (typically 15th of each month)
- Minimum payout threshold: $1 USD (varies by country)

**Paid apps may need additional information:**

### Step 7: Consider Free Trial or Refund Policy

**Options for Paid Apps:**

**Free Trial (Optional):**
- Offer 7-day free trial
- Users can try before buying
- Can increase conversions

**Refund Policy:**
- Google's default: 48-hour refund window
- Users can request refunds automatically
- You can extend refund window up to 30 days

**To Set Up:**
- Go to: Play Console → Your App → Policy → Refund policy
- Configure refund window
- Set up free trial (if desired)

## Pricing Strategy Considerations

### Factors to Consider:

1. **Market Research:**
   - Check competitor pricing
   - Chess apps typically: $0.99 - $4.99
   - Premium apps: $4.99 - $9.99

2. **Value Proposition:**
   - What makes your app worth paying for?
   - Ad-free experience
   - Complete feature set
   - Regular updates
   - Privacy-focused

3. **Target Audience:**
   - Casual players: Lower price ($0.99 - $1.99)
   - Serious players: Higher price ($2.99 - $4.99)
   - Niche market: Premium price ($4.99+)

4. **Launch Strategy:**
   - Start higher, can lower later
   - Start lower, can raise later (harder)
   - Consider launch discount (e.g., 50% off first week)

## Revenue Expectations

### Realistic Expectations:

**Paid apps typically have:**
- **Lower download volume** (10-100x less than free)
- **Higher user engagement** (users who pay are more committed)
- **Better reviews** (paying users expect quality)

**Example Scenarios:**

**Scenario 1: $0.99 app**
- 100 downloads/month = ~$70/month revenue
- 1,000 downloads/month = ~$700/month revenue

**Scenario 2: $1.99 app**
- 50 downloads/month = ~$70/month revenue
- 500 downloads/month = ~$700/month revenue

**Scenario 3: $2.99 app**
- 30 downloads/month = ~$63/month revenue
- 300 downloads/month = ~$630/month revenue

**Note:** These are rough estimates. Actual numbers vary widely based on:
- App quality
- Marketing
- Competition
- Reviews and ratings
- App Store optimization

## Quick Reference: Paid App Setup

### Required Steps:

1. ✅ Set up Merchant Account (Play Console → Settings → Payments)
2. ✅ Provide tax information
3. ✅ Add bank account
4. ✅ Wait for verification (1-3 days)
5. ✅ Create app as "Paid" (not "Free")
6. ✅ Set price tier
7. ✅ Update store listing for paid positioning
8. ✅ Configure refund policy
9. ✅ Submit app

### Revenue Calculation:

```
Revenue per sale = Price × 0.70 (after Google's 30% commission)
Monthly revenue = Revenue per sale × Number of sales
```

### Example:
- Price: $1.99
- Your cut: $1.39 per sale
- 100 sales/month = $139/month
- 1,000 sales/month = $1,390/month
